CT & FSM domain demo - Thermostat System

Introduction

This applet shows a simple thermostat system. The temperature of the room is expected to be controlled between 0 and 0.2

The system has two states, heating and cooling, as shown in the figure below. In the heating state, the temperature of the room is increased linearly, in terms of a differential equation: 

    dx/dt = 1
In the cooling state, the temperature is dropped linearly, i.e. 
    dx/dt = -1
The control rule is that if the temperature reaches 0.2 degree, then swich the controller to the cooling state; if the temperature decreases to 0 degree then switch the controller to the heating state.

We use this demo to illustrate the accuracy of detecting events, and the ability of simulating hybrid system in Ptolemy II.
