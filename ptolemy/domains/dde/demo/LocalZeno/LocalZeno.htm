Localized Zeno Conditions in DDE Models

The above applet illustrates the localization of Zeno conditions in the DDE domain. The demo consists of two topologically identical branches connected to a clock source actor. Each branch contains a feedback loop and is fed identical tokens from the clock source. As with all feedback loops in DDE models, a time delay is encountered as tokens feedback through the loops. It has been shown that in discrete event systems (both distributed and centralized) the feedback time delay must be greater than a positive constant value or else a Zeno condition will be experienced.

In centralized discrete event systems, Zeno conditions prevent progress in the entire simulation model. In distributed systems, Zeno conditions are localized as much as is possible based on the topology of the system because DDE models naturally separate unnecessary dependencies in subcomponents of the model.

In the above model, the bottom branch experiences a Zeno condition. This causes the the bottom branch to continue execution long after the top branch has ceased execution, even though the clock source sends identical data to both branches.
